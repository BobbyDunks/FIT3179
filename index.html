Millenials, Houses and Avocados

In 2015, Tim Gurner, a housing mogul, Declared that millenials can't afford houses because they are spending all their money on avocados. In this dashboard, I'm going to investigate if there is any truth to that claim. To do this, I am going to address each of these four questions: Where are the Millenials? How many Millenials are there? Are they buying the most avocados? And finally, Are avocados spendings affecting their housing prospects?

Where are the Millenials?

This first visualisation shows the percentage of the population of each state made up by the millenial generation. While there is unfortunately a lot of missing data, we can see that there is a tentative trend for western, coastal states to have a higher percentage of millenials.

How Many Millenials Are There?

In this stacked bar chart, we can see a more detailed breakdown of the state demographics. Millenials are highlighted in green. The difference between them is too hard to compare by eye, but by playing with the sliders, we can better appreciate the different demographics of the high, mid and low percentage millenial states.

Are They Buying The Most Avocados?

In this visualisaition we can see the number of avocados purchased per capita in each state. By keeping the same colour scheme from the first visualisation we can easily see how millenial heavy each state is. Look at those results! According to Tim Gurner, we should be seeing the most millenial-heavy states buying the most avocados, but instead we see the opposite! The top three avocado loving states are in the lowest millenial category! Not looking good for Tim Gurner...

Is it Affecting Their Housing Prospects?

Finally, we take a look at how much each state spends on avocados per year and compare that against their respective average housing price. The size of each dot represents the number of years that an average person in that state would need to stop buying avocados to afford a house in their state. If Tim Gurner is correct, we should expect to see that all the high-millenial states would be small, given that avocados are supposedly the sole obstacle between millenials and home ownership. Instead, we see that the only states in the smallest years to buy bracket are all in the lowest quantile of millenial population!

The only explanation of the data examined here is that Tim Gurner must have slightly mised the mark with his predictions. It turns out that there are more factors at play in Millenial's struggle for homeownership than simply avo indulgence... who knew?
